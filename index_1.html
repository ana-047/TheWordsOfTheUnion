History


The tradition of the State of the Union (SOTU) address dates back to the early days of the United States when President George Washington delivered the first annual message to Congress in 1790.

Over the centuries, the speech has evolved into a crucial annual event, providing Presidents with a platform to address the nation, communicate their policy priorities, and fulfill the constitutional obligation to update Congress on the state of affairs.

Impact


The State of the Union address holds significant impact, serving as a key moment for the President to set the policy agenda, address national concerns, and outline a vision for the future.

Beyond its constitutional mandate, the SOTU plays a pivotal role in shaping public perception, influencing legislative priorities, and fostering communication between the executive and legislative branches, ultimately leaving a lasting imprint on the trajectory of the nation.

Allies and Adversaries: A history of international shout-outs

In recent years, the focus of historical State of the Union addresses has shifted from prominent mentions of Mexico, a neighbor, and Great Britain, a longstanding ally, to an emphasis on American adversaries and countries with which the United States has engaged in conflicts. Over the past decade, China, Afghanistan, Iran, Russia, Syria, Cuba, and North Korea have prominently featured in these addresses, marking a notable shift in the geopolitical landscape addressed by U.S. presidents.

A complex interplay of political, diplomatic, and rhetorical considerations may be the reason why adversaries have been mentioned more than allies. Since one of the objectives of the SOTU is addressing imminent challenges, adversarial relations, conflicts, or international tensions are likely to be significant challenges that presidents feel compelled to address. In some cases, addressing adversaries could be a priority, either to emphasize the need for diplomatic resolutions, to highlight threats, rally public support for particular actions or demonstrate leadership and resolve.
